About me

Dr. Pan Zhang is a distinguished civil engineer with a robust academic background and rich experience in research and industry. His expertise encompasses structural engineering, shell theory, computational mechanics and FRP composites.

He holds an Honors Bachelor of Engineering degree from Zhejiang University's Chu Kochen Honors College. He earned a Msc in Civil Engineering from Northwestern University, USA, and a PhD in from the Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU), under the supervision of Prof. Jin-Guang Teng, the president of the university.

As a Postdoctoral Fellow at PolyU, he has worked on innovative projects, including the development of lightweight multifunctional noise barriers using smart FRP composites and the study of precast seawater and sea-sand self-compacting concrete (SSSW-SCC) structures. Prior roles included Research Associate and Research Assistant, where he contributed to studies on square concrete-filled steel tubular columns and the stability of elliptical cylindrical shells
